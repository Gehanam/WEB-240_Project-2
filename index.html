What was the Legacy of the Exmouth View Hotel?

The Exmouth View Hotel was a historic establishment that served the community for many years. It was known for its hospitality and the quality of its services. It was built in 1926 with venetian era architecture concepts. The area around the hotel, Torquay, Deven, UK, has served local area for beach vaction spots and was an obervation post during 2 major World wars. During World War 2 in preperation for D-Day, the invasion of Frances Normandy beaches, the Exmouth View Hotel was use to house military members.

The Offerings of the Exmouth View Hotel

There were 25 rooms available for singles, couples, and families. Of these rooms 12 offered ocean views and 3 with balcony views. Just a note about the room arrangements, they were not exactly in order based on floor arrangement, more upon style of the room. One of the key specials was offering two packages with check in times starting Monday to Friday and a weekend over Friday to Monday. Should a couple have choosen to stay during these times was a reduced rate at competive rate per night vs booking outside of these days. Each room was moderized but kept the orgins of victorian architecture. This was reported by some guests in a positive or negative light. The area of Torquay was a popular destination for tourists and locals but isnt a college party town destination.

Who doesnt like food included in the price of your stay?

Typically, the restruant venue menu would be included in with ammenties but in this case deserves its own section. Though the years the Exmoth View Hotel remained open, its menu choices for guests improved over time. As items are added to food options it does increase food costs yet is essential for guests to try new meals during thier visit or return holiday trips. It offered chicken, beef, duck, pork, and fish fresh from local fisheriers. Another distinguished aspect was vegan dishes to meet larger client audiance. After reading the menu my stomoach grumbled, even after just eating, as the hot and cold desserts finish off a quality meal.

The reasons why the Exmouth View Hotel closed

Upon review of historical records and review of the hotel I found the website was inconsistently maintained. This is once cause but not the leading factor for its closure. The impact of Covid 19 with its travel restrictions hurt many local and family owned businesses. The owner, Dave Browne, was affected by lack of tourism with rising costs of maintaining the structure and would assume cost of food on menu avaliable to guests. The Exmouth View Hotel was sold 30 Sept 2019 in a decison inconjunction with Development Management Committee to allow construction of 12 apartments and two town houses with associated parking.
